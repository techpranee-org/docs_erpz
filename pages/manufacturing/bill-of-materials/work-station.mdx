# Workstation

**A Workstation stores information regarding the place where the workstation operations are carried out.**

Data regarding the operation costs, rent, and electricity can be stored here.

**Note:** An operation can take place at multiple Workstations.

An Operation takes place at a Workstation. The Operation is the work performed, and the Workstation is the place/machine where it is performed. For example, melting is an Operation that can be done at 10 different Workstations.

To access the Workstation list, go to:

`Home > Manufacturing > Bill of Materials > Workstation`

## 1. How to Create a Workstation 

To create a Workstation, follow these steps:

1. Go to the Workstation list and click on **New**.
2. Enter a name for the Workstation.
3. Under **Operating Costs**, enter the following as applicable:
   - Electricity Cost
   - Rent Cost
   - Consumable Cost
   - Wages
4. Save.

Optionally, you can enter a description for the Workstation.

You can add the hours when the Workstation will be operational. By adding a holiday list, the days listed as holidays won't be counted as working for the Workstation.

## 2. Features 

### 2.1 Production Capacity 
Production Capacity is the total number of jobs that can be executed simultaneously in the respective Workstation.

![Work Order]()

### 2.2 Working Hours 
In the Working Hours table, you can add start and end times for a Workstation. For example, a Workstation may be active from 9 AM to 1 PM, then from 2 PM to 5 PM. You can also specify the working hours based on shifts. While scheduling a Work Order, the system will check for the availability of the Workstation based on the working hours specified.

### 2.3 Holiday List 
A Holiday List can be added to exclude these days from being counted as working days for the Workstation. 

**Note:** You can enable overtime for a Workstation in Manufacturing Settings.
